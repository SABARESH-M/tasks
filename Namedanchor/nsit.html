Sarathy, was born in Salem on 14th November 1931. Founder of Smt. Mahalakshmi Ammal Educational Trust and Narasu's Sarathy Institute of Technology, He developed his early work experiences in many different enterprises like a provision store, cosmetic store, cloth store and had also worked as a booking clerk in a cinema theatre. During this time he also travelled 20 – 30 miles on a bicycle to sell roasted coffee powder. In 1949, he started a coffee shop, “Sarathy Coffee” which had the facility to roast powder and package the raw coffee seeds as filter coffee.

He was the Founder President for Sri Vidya Mandir Schools and introduced the CBSE system of educa- tion to Salem. In 2010 when Vidya Mandir Trust laid its foundation for an Arts College he was requested to be the Chairman and the college is successfully functioning at present. He was also the Founder Chairman of Vysya Group of Institution and remained in that post till his demise. He was instrumental in upgrading the Library and establishing the Audio visual Hall in Sri Sarada College for Women, Salem.

ABOUT US

In 1966, he acquired Narasu's Coffee Company at the age of 35, with a mindset to turn things around in the coffee industry and since then there was no looking back. In a short span of time, the sales increased from 300 – 400 metric tons to 5700 metric tons per year. He simultaneously devel- oped coffee plantations which supplied raw coffee for his own company and was open to sales in the market. In short, under his supervision, coffee was grown, cultivated, roasted, ground, packaged and marketed with high degree of quality control. This could be achieved only because of the dedication, hard work, honesty, business ethics and a good market understanding. Hence, the brand “Narasu's” became popular in the Indian and International markets.

The business knowledge of Sri “Narasu's” R.P. Sarathy was so vast that the entire coffee industry relied on his suggestions. He served as a Coffee Board Trade member for seven terms (21 years). One of his notable achievements during this tenure was to make coffee into a free market. As an effect of this resolu- tion, the purchase and sales of coffee became competitive and in par with the quality of the product. With his experience in the coffee industry he diversified into various other industries under the brand name “Narasu's”. They are: Narasu's Coffee Company, Narasu's Exports, Narasu's Roller Flour Mills, Narasu's Spinning Mills, Narasu's Sarathy Jewellery, Narasu's Sarathy Institute of Technology, Narasu's Sarathy Industries, Mahalakshmi Oil Refineries, Coffee Estates, Hotels.
